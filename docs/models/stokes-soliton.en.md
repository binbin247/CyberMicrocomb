# Stokes soliton

[中文](./stokes-soliton.md)

## Simulation equations

This model uses a normalized two-field coupled LLE for the primary field
$P(\phi,t)$ and the Stokes field $S(\phi,t)$. The linear operators are
applied in the frequency domain:

$$
\left.\frac{\partial \hat P_\mu}{\partial t}\right|_{\mathrm{lin}}
=
\left(-1-i\alpha_P-id_{2P}\mu^2\right)\hat P_\mu,
$$

$$
\left.\frac{\partial \hat S_\mu}{\partial t}\right|_{\mathrm{lin}}
=
\left(-1-i\alpha_S-id_{2S}\mu^2-i\delta\mu\right)\hat S_\mu.
$$

In the current UI, $\alpha_S=0$ is fixed and is not user adjustable. The nonlinear
and Raman-coupling terms are applied in the time domain:

$$
N_P =
i|P|^2
-i\tau_R\left(\partial_\phi |P|^2+\eta\partial_\phi |S|^2\right)
+\eta\left[i(2-f_R)-\frac{g_P}{2}\right]|S|^2,
$$

$$
N_S =
ir_w|S|^2
-ir_w\tau_R\left(\eta\partial_\phi |P|^2+\partial_\phi |S|^2\right)
+\eta r_w\left[i(2-f_R)+\frac{g_S}{2}\right]|P|^2.
$$

Here $\eta$ is the mode overlap, $f_R$ is the Raman fraction, $g_P$ is the
primary Raman loss, $g_S$ is the Stokes Raman gain, and $r_w$ is the wavelength
ratio. The primary field receives the external pump $F$. The Stokes field starts
from a weak complex-noise seed and is amplified by Raman gain.

## Physical picture

A Stokes soliton is a second soliton field generated by Raman interaction with a
primary soliton. The primary soliton first forms a strong localized field in the
pumped mode family; that localized field provides Raman gain to the Stokes mode
family. The Stokes field grows from a weak seed and becomes temporally trapped in
the optical potential shared with the primary pulse.

The Stokes soliton is therefore not a direct copy of the primary soliton. Its
intensity, spectrum, and drift are controlled by Raman gain/loss, overlap, FSR
mismatch, Stokes dispersion, and wavelength ratio. With the default preset, the
main interactive route is to scan `Pump detuning` and watch the transition from a
primary soliton to a Stokes soliton.

## Demo

1. Select `Stokes soliton` in `MODEL`.
2. Use the defaults: `grid = 1024`, `Pump detuning = 39.1`,
   `Pump power = 12.247`, `Primary D2 = 0.02`, `Stokes D2 = 0.02`,
   `FSR mismatch = 0`, `Overlap = 0.5`, `fR = 0.18`,
   `Primary Raman loss = 0.126`, `Stokes Raman gain = 0.126`,
   `Wavelength ratio = 1550/1630`, `tauR = 0.00033`,
   `Noise seed = 0.00001`, `stepsPerFrame = 1000`.
3. Click `Play`.
4. Compare the primary and Stokes intensity profiles in `Temporal field`.
5. Use `Temporal evolution` to watch both fields evolve; the Stokes field usually
   grows from a weak noisy background.
6. Scan `Pump detuning` to explore the primary-to-Stokes soliton transition.

If the Stokes field stays near the noise floor, increase `Stokes Raman gain`
slightly or let the simulation run longer. If the simulation becomes slow, reduce
`stepsPerFrame` or the grid size first.
